                        The Leap of Faith 
                       by Taliesin Pendragon
               Delivered by UID courier: Brnghurst 
                       (aka David K Goodman)
 
The Leap of Faith is an ancient abandoned mine shaft high in the
Shartans, from which one can exit by only two routes: a chute that
takes you safely to the bottom of the mountain, and a broken chute
that leaves you dead and mangled at the bottom.  The only way to know
which chute to take is the word of the person ahead of you.
 
The entrance to the Leap is an archway blocked by a misshapen
boulder.  You enter the leap by pushing the boulder, which can only
be done if there is no-one behind it. Once inside, the boulder rolls 
back into place, blocking your exit.  There are three openings, one
of which is randomly blocked by the boulder.  This leaves only two
openings, and you don't know which order they are in.  You can't see
what's beyond each opening.  Climbing the boulder takes you to a
ledge above this tight shaft.
 
From the ledge, you can see which opening leads to which chute. The
only exit from the ledge is down. You can only go down if no-one is
in the room below you.  While you are on the ledge, some-one can
enter the room below.  While some-one is on the ledge, no-one else
can climb the boulder.  You locate the openings by searching.  If the 
boulder is moved, you must search again to see if the openings have
changed.
 
You can't MARK the ledge or the room below, nor Teleport or Bend
Space out, nor use Mist or other forms to fool the rooms.
 
A lone person can enter and climb the boulder to determine which
opening is which, then climb down and select one.  They may choose to 
take a fast, fun ride to the bottom, or kill themselves, or not look
first and blindly pick one, casting their fate to the gods.
 
A person leading another must communicate the situation to the one
who follows them in.  They can only leave once the person below
chooses an opening.  The person below must trust the person above
them.  Some guilds or assocations may make use of this situation to
test the faith of their followers or new initiates.
 
The archway is somewhere high in the Shartans.  Both chutes leave you 
at the top of the wall in the gobs, but in different conditions of 
health <grin>.  It would be nice if the person on the ledge could 
observe the room below.  It would be even nicer if there were an 
observation room above the ledge from which people could observe the 
ledge and the lower room.
 
Another aspect of this setup can occur during a pursuit.  If a person 
is being stalked or followed, they can step into the Leap.  If the 
pursuer follows, it become a whole different situation.  If the two 
people are less than friendly, then the question becomes, should the 
one on the ledge give good or bad directions, and should the person 
below trust them or not?

; INSIDE THE LEAP OF FAITH **********************************************
 
> look
[The Leap of Faith]
You are in a tight shaft, with room for one person only. A misshapen
boulder is blocking your exit through the archway you came through.
There are two openings, but from this vantage point nothing can be
seen of what lays beyond. 
Obvious exits: none. 
 
; SAFE CHUTE
> go x opening        ; x is either 1 or 2
You go through the opening and find yourself in an icy chute.  Your
body descends at high speed, exhilirating in the speed as the wind
whistles past your head. The sights are just a blur as you carom from 
turn to turn. Finally, your reach an upturn which slows your descent, 
and you fly off the end of the chute, landing gently at the feet of
the Shartans. 
 
; BROKEN CHUTE
You go through the opening and find yourself in an icy chute.  Your
body descends at high speed, exhilirating in the speed as the wind
whistles past your head. The sights are just a blur as you carom from 
turn to turn. Then, you gasp in disbelief when you notice a broken
gap in the chute! Your body flails wildly for a moment before it
augers into the dirt at the feet of the Shartans. You'd be spitting
dirt except for one thing: you're dead. 
 
 
> go archway
You can't go there!
 
; WITH SOMEONE ON A LEDGE
> climb boulder
Something prevents you from climbing the boulder.
 
; WITH NO-ONE ON A LEDGE
> climb boulder
You carefully climb the boulder, reaching a ledge above.
 
 
> look
[Ledge above a Boulder]
From this lofty perch you behold a breathtaking view of the land and
sea below you.  Wind blows crisply past your head, making your eyes
water.  You see two openings in the shaft below you.
Obvious exits: down.
 
> search
The first opening you see leads to an icy chute leading to the bottom 
of the mountain.  The second leads to a broken chute which drops off
into space. 
 
; WITH SOME-ONE BELOW YOU
> down
Something prevents you from climbing down the boulder.
 
; WITH NO-ONE BELOW YOU
> down
You climb down the boulder to the room below.
 
 
                       DISCOVERED MANUSCRIPT
 
When the rooms are first discovered, I'd like the following decaying
manuscript to be found within (which may be removed by the finder): 
 
 On (a date 3 years ago Old Calendar), my son, Taliesin Pendragon,
 died at the hands of another.  Lest he be forgotten, I've committed
 his story to parchment.  It starts with a girl ... Mariquilla. 
 
 Mariquilla was a lovely young woman.  Tall and slender she was, with 
 long, flowing hair and a quick smile.  She loved to wander alone in
 the woods, reveling in the beauty and mysteries of nature.  Although 
 a talented weaver with no fighting skills, she had no fear of the
 creatures she found, and they never bothered her. She was often seen 
 in remote locations, from the Inner Sea to the high Shartans. 
 
 From the first time he saw Mariquilla, Taliesin was smitten with
 her.  His love of nature and knowledge of the wilds made them
 instant friends, and they became inseparable.  Fayd's maidens were
 sad at the loss of another bachelor, but the love Mari and Tali
 shared brought joy to the hearts of all. 
 
 The tragedy began when Taliesin was called to aid his cousins in a
 far-off village.  Shield in hand, he kissed Mariquilla farewell and
 left to defend his kinfolk.  For six months, he battled against the
 creatures that threatened that village.  When at last they had
 succeeded in driving off the attackers, a weary Taliesin returned to 
 Fayd.  In his absence, however, Mari had fallen for another man. 
 
 In her travels, Mariquilla had discovered an ancient mine shaft high 
 in the Shartans.  She brought Taliesin to that shaft, and there she 
 broke the news and his heart in one stroke.  He vowed to pursue her
 to the ends of Andor, and to slay all who might try to keep her from
 him.  It was this she had feared.  Only two openings led from the 
 shaft he was in, and from a ledge above, Mari could tell him which 
 opening led to safety.  She advised him to enter the opening to the 
 left, and trusting her, he took it.
 
 Taliesin's quick plunge down an icy chute was followed by disaster
 as he flew off the end of the broken chute, falling through space to 
 land in a heap at the feet of the Shartans.  His screams of rage and
 anguish as he fell had been heard echoing all the way to the City
 Gate.  Travellers from Grymwood had seen him fall, but by the time
 they reached the bottom, nothing was left but a pool of blood.  His
 body is presumed to have been dragged off by animals. 
 
 My wife, gods rest her soul, had been taken from us not long before.  
 I am grateful she did not live to see Taliesin's untimely fall, and
 pray that he is with her now.  The people of Fayd have spoken of
 sealing the mine shaft, but I would rather that it stand as a
 monument to my son.  Remember him.
