By The Power Vested In Him By Shemri, Amilor, Rorin And
Kaliban Upon His Day Of Coronation, The King Is The Law.
Whatsoever He Surveys Is His Realm.  Herein Are The His
Laws Of Fayd, Governing Citizens Within The City Walls,
Including The Graveyard And Catacombs And Having No
Jurisdiction Without, Unless The King Does Specifically
Instruct:

The Crimes Punishable In Fayd By Warrant Number:

  I:   Petty Theft: Filching Non-artifact Items Worth Less
       Than 100 Gold. This includes items left on the ground
        when crimianls are arrested.

 II:   Grand Theft: Filching Non-artifact Items Worth 100
       gold or more but less than 1,000 gold. This includes
       items left on the ground when criminals are arrested.

III:   Minor Dark Necromancy: The Casting Of Body Destruction
       Spells and Wail of the Banshee.

 IV:   Fraud: Claiming To Be A Known Citizen Of Fayd Whom
       You Are Not Or Claiming To Hold Government, Official,
       Clerical And/Or Royal Titles And Positions That You
       Do Not, Otherwise, disguise is not a crime.

  V:   Breaking And Entering: Using Force, Vandalism Or
       Stealth To Enter Into An Area Not Belonging To You,
       Or Where You Are Unwanted Or Prohibited.

 VI:   Assault: An Action Taken Against Someone That
       Threatens Their Life, Liberty Or Property, Or
       Otherwise Makes Them Subject To Unwanted Dweomers,
       Or Persistant Sexual Harrassment And/Or Unwelcomed
       Physical Advances. (Once A Complaint Of Sexual
       Harrassment Has Been Filed With A Lawkeeper
       Continuing Such Behaviors Will Result In Assault
       Charges.)

       Heresy: Speaking The Name Of The Devourer Of Souls In
       Public, Or Taking The Name Of Shemri, Amilor, Rorin
       And/Or Kaliban In Vain, Or Defiling The Temples Of
       Such Or Other Forms Of Sacralidge And Blasphemy.

       Kidnapping: Dragging, Carrying, Dominating or
       otherwise forceable taking a citizen from the City or
       Anywhere with the intent to do them harm

VII:   Major DARK Necromancy: Summoning, Consorting With Or
       Creating Undead (This Includes Controlling Of Undead
       Except For The Purposes Of Banishing Them), Or
       Summoning Entities Whose Presence In The Realms Is
       A Threat To Public Safety, Or Bringing Dangerous
       Dominated Entities Into the City.

       Aiding Or Abetting: Hiding The Whereabouts Of,
       Healing,  Or Casting Helpful Spells On Warranted
       Individuals, Or Guarding Them In Order To
       Block Their Arrest.

       Grand Theft II: Filching A Unique Artifact Or Item
       Worth More Than 1,000 Gold. This includes items left
       on the ground when criminals are arrested.

VIII:  Murder: The Slaying Of A Citizen Or Visitor In Fayd.
       Self Preservation is a defense to the crime of murder
       if the response is a reasonable one The Highest Ranking
       Member Of A Temple Or Guild  Present Has The Right To
       Defend Or Seek Aid In The Defense Of The Temple Or Guild
       Property Against Intruders Once Warning Is Given.
       Death As The Result Of A Consentual Duel Shall Not Be
       Considered Murder.

IX:    Treason: Acting Against Or Obstructing The Agents Of
       Law, Order, Government And/Or The Crown In Fayd.
       Assaulting A Lawkeeper Or Royal Guard.

There Must Be At Least Two Witnesses Other Than The Victim
To Warrant Someone For Crime III Or Above (They Need Not Be
Named Citizens. People Who Bear Witness From Behind Windows
Or Who Are Among The Crowds On The Street Are Adequate Witnesses).

In Cases Of Multiple or Repeated Murders, and Treason, Or
Incidents Where Banishment or Obliteration Are In Order, Trials
May Be Held At The Whim Of The King, Wherein He Shall Appoint A
Jury Of Five. If the King Is Not Available, Or Unwilling, The
Highest Ranking Lawkeeper Should Escort The Acused Outside of
the City Gates for Banishment.

Banished Individuals Who Illegally Enter The City and Are Caught
Committing More Crimes Will Be Subject To Permanent Death.

The Sentences That May Be Imposed By An Authorized Judge Of
Fayd, In Order Of Severity Include: Fines, Single Death,
Banishment From The City, Obliteration (Permanent Death).
Obliteration Is Reserved Only For Repeating The Most Hideous
Of Crimes.
