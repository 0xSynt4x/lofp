sing The wind blew hot across the dunes,\And all before it scattered.
sing Among those things there was a man,\A man whose mind had shattered.
sing He was born anew amonst the palms,\An oasis of sweet water.
sing Brought back to life there in the arms,\Of the wise man's daughter.
sing There he would have ever stayed,\Until the sun grew cold.
sing But a hot wind wrecked the plans he'd made,\It blew him from the fold.
sing Another oasis did he find,\And with it yet another mind.
sing But the old oasis drew him back,\Two homes had he, and time did lack.
sing Each oasis had charms to draw him there,\But to neither oasis was he fair.
sing A man must make one place his home,\Else waste his life between.
sing But he continued to roam, and lost a home,\Its waters had receded.
sing He mourns the loss of one so fair,\But rejoices in his new-found lair.
sing Back to the first the did go,\Back to the wise man's daughter.
sing Now the man basks by the warm palms,\And drinks of the Sweet Water.
