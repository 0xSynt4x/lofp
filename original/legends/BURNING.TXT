     Once More into the Breech

Flames shoot out their fiery rockets,
Embers burn eyes in weeping sockets.
She stands before me, burnt and damaged,
Eyeing the house the flames have ravaged.

Within she thinks her love is waiting,
A chance so slim it's aggravating.
I bid her stay, would bar the door,
But know that love means so much more.

The smoke and hissing beckons her dare,
Of her own free will to leave the air.
Inside I fear she'll lose the fight,
Her heart consumed and lost from sight.

What can I do? I must go in,
To forsake her is its own foul sin.
If it's love not smoke I'll help her find it,
If the smoke ensnares I must unwind it.

So in she goes and I then follow,
A lump in throat I cannot swallow.
Our lives will never be the same,
If only tears could quench the flame.
