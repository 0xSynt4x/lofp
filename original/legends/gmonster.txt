Hiya Dave. Just wanted to give you a bit of scripting information. Again, 
I KNOW you're gonna have a ton of questions, because SCRIPT.DOC doesn't 
even cover a third of what you need to know. So please, if you can't find 
a way to do something, don't assume that you CAN'T do it. Because you 
probably can. Just ask me or Danna. Anyway, the purpose of this email is 
to tell you about the token characters used to echo certain names and 
pronouns...

%n = Name of active player
%h = his/her/its
%s = she/he/it
%i = him/her/it
%p = Name of active player when a group (ect. Dave's group)
%a = Name of active item (plus its adjectives and extended text)
%m = Name of monster
%c = a return

If you use those above with an uppercase letter, then you get the 
uppercase versions of them.

These are used with the ECHO command. And here is a list of what you can
ECHO to...

ECHO PLAYER (sends text to active player)
ECHO OTHERS (sends text to everyone but the active player)
ECHO GROUP (sends text to active player and everyone in his group)
ECHO OTHGROUP (send text to everyone but the active player and his group)
ECHO ALL (sends text to everyone in the room)

Also, if you want to affect another room, you just AFFECT <room number> 
and then your script starts affecting THAT room.

Okay, you just asked me about creating an item in a script. If you wanted 
to create the item in the active player's inventory, you'd do this...

NEWITEM -1 <archnum>

Basically the same way you create an item in the room. But instead of
assigning it a number of 0 - 9, you use -1 to put it in the player's 
inventory.

But let's say you wanted to create the item, but just have it IN the room.
The only way to do that IS to assign it a number of 0 - 9. So you could 
just check to see if the room you're in as a vacant item slot, and if so, 
use it. The way you'd do that is to see if item 9 is defined....

IFNOITEM 9
   NEWITEM 9 <archnum>
ELSE
   ECHO PLAYER There's not enough room here.
ENDIF

Monster Scripting
-----------------

Monster Adjectives:

Adjectives for monsters are difined similar to, but not the same as they 
are for items. Everyone must be assigned numbers for monster adjectives. 
To define a monster adjective you use MADJDEF # <adjective>.

Example:
MADJDEF 10 giant

NAME <name text>
Defines the name for the monster. Do not use a capital letter to start the
monsters name unless you are making it a UNIQUE monster.

ADJECTIVE #
Defines the adjective for the monster. Do not use Adjectives with Unique
monsters. This number is defined by the MADJ(monster adjectives).

HUMAN
This is a macro that defines the creatures body parts as humanoid.

AVINE
Defines the creatures body parts as birdlike.

ANIMAL
Defines a creatures body parts as a quadripedal animal.

PART #
This can be used in conjunction with or alone to define body parts on
creatures. The available body parts are as follows:

0 = head
1 = body
2 = back
3 = second head
4 = thorax
5 = right arm
6 = left arm
7 = right leg
8 = left leg
9 = right hind leg
10 = left hind leg
11 = right foreleg
12 = left foreleg
13 = right hand
14 = left hand
15 = right wing
16 = left wing
17 = tail
18 = right paw
19 = left paw


Body #
Defines the number of body points that are factored into the exp value of 
the monster.

EXTRABODY
Defines an added amount of body point between 50-100% of the actual number
that is NOT factored into the exp value of the monster.

ATTACK1 #
Defines the monsters attack modifier of the creatures primary attack.

ATTACK2 #
Defines the attack modifier of the creatures secondary attack. This is not
used at the moment.

Defense #
Defines the monsters defend modifier.

STRATEGY #
This defines how the monster reacts in combat and overall behavior. The
following strategies are available.

1-100 Non-hostile, flee when attacked. (note: if 1-25, the creature will
also cry for help and alert legal authorities)

101-200 Non-hostile, defend themselves when attacked. 101-125 will cry for
help and alert legal authorities.

201-300 Animals that always flee even if not attacked but defend 
themselves if attacked.

301-400 Hostile, flee when wounded. (group A)

401-500 Hostile, flee when wounded. (group B)

501-600 Hostile, fight to the death. (group A)

601-700 Hostile, fight to the death. (group B)

There is no destinction between group A and B at this time. The intention 
of the difference in the future will be that Group A and group B will be 
mutually hostile.

TREASURE #
Defines the treasure level for the creature. If this is zero, the creature
has no treasure.

Immunity (immunity #) (immunity level)
This defines a monster has having immunity to certain forms of attack. Or 
in some cases extra susceptability to certain forms of attack. Immunity 
level defines how susceptable or immune it is to that form of attack in 
half-units. 2 is the default setting which means the creature is affected 
2/2 (in full). 1 would be half damage. 3 would be 1 and 1/2 damage and 4 
would be double damage. All immunities, unless noted otherwise, are set to 
the default 2 except for #8 which is set to 0.


The available immunities are:

0 - Ensnaring attacks, particularly large or supernatural creatures would
ignore this form of attack.

1 - Crushing weapons.

2 - Edged weapons.

3 - Heat attacks.

4 - Electrical attacks.

5 - Cold attacks.

6 - Nervous system attacks. Creature would be immune to spells like charm,
sleep, psionic blast, etc. due to the lack of a normal nervous system.

7 - Life affecting. creatures would be immune to body destruction, poison,
disease and body restoration due to a lack of an active "life force" or 
are to unlike normal life.

8 - Undead affecting. This is the only immunity that is default to 0 and 
it must be set to a special value. When set to 3 it defines a creature as 
a lesser undead, and includes creatures up to and including a lesser 
wight. Setting it to 2 defines it as a greater undead such as a mummy, 
lesser vampire, etc. Setting it to 1 should be reserved for extremely 
powerful undead such as a lich, a greater vampire, etc.

9 - Susceptability to death criticals. If set to 0 the creature would not
receive a fancy death critical. This should be given to any creature that
lacks a normal humanoid or animal physiology.


RACE #
Defines the creatures racial group. At the moment this is only used in
conjunction with certain VALue settings on weapons for weapons of slaying.
The available races are as follows:

21 - extraplanar, creatures that come from other planes.

22 - Undead

23 - Arachnid

24 - Kvantar

25 - Troll

26 - Lycanthrope

27 - Giant

28 - Constructs (either magical or technological)

29 - Goblin

30 - Ogre

31 - Dragon

WEAPON (weapon#) (adjective#) (weighted probability)
This defines the type of weapon the creature uses. Weapon# is the 
archetype # for the weapon. Adjective defines a standard adjective for the 
weapon and the weighted probability is the percent out of 100 that the 
creature attacks with this weapon.

WEAPONPLUS #
Defines the creatures weapon as magical with # being the bonus of the 
weapon. For obvious reasons you want to be carefull not to set this value 
to high.


ARMOR #
Defines the percentage value of damage reduction that the monster 
receives.

MAGICWEAPON (1, 2 or 3)
If this is defined, then a magic weapon is needed to damage the creature. 
If MAGICWEAPON 1, then any magic weapon will do. 2 requires a bonus of at 
least +10. 3 requires a bonus of at least +21.

SKINADJ <#>
Defines the standard adjective number to attach to any items skinned from 
a creature.

SKINITEM <item#> <probability> <coppervalue> <magic>
Item# defines the archetype number of a certain skin that is available on 
a givenr monster. The "probability" is another weighted probability 
similar to that used for distibuting weapon selection. "Coppervalue is the 
items average value in copper. "Magic is a number thatwhich specifies the 
magic content of the item. If 0 which most skins should be, the item is 
non-magical. Otherwise it is a spell component. Some item archetypes you 
may use for creature skins are: 504 = beard 505 = claw 506 = scalp 507 = 
scale 508 = ear 509 = pelt 510 = tooth 512 = dust 513 = talon 514 = hide 
515 = hair 516 = beak 517 = feathers 518 = paw 519 = horn 520 = eye 521 = 
tongue 522 = tail 523 = skin 524 = tusk 525 = finger 526 = toe 527 = leg 
528 = fangs


Discorporate
This indicates that if the monster is killed it will not leave a corpse
behind.

ETERNAL
This indicates that the creature will not be unloaded by the system until 
it is killed.

GENDER <0, 1 or 2>
The default is 2. 0=Male, 1=Female

STEALABLE
This indicates the monster may be stolen from.

POISON <% chance> <x>
If defined, this provides a percent chance that a level x poison is 
delivered to the victim upon being struck.

DISEASE <% chance> <x>
This is basically the same as POISON.

FATIGUE <%chance> <x>
This is the equivalent of a life draining attack.

RESIST <x>
This defines the creatures magic resistance.

SPEED <x>
The default setting is 3. It defines how fast the creature attacks and 
moves. 1 is fastest and 4 or more is for extremely slow creatures.

*DESCRIPTION_START
Blah blah blah
*DESCRIPTION_END
Standard description field just like it is used for rooms.


HIDESKILL <X>
This defines a percentage chance that the creature will be able to hide. 
it is also used to define the creatures skill at backstab. X is the base 
chance. Warning: do not define X above 100.

GUARD <X>
defines what creature this monster will guard where X is the monster 
number that is to be guarded.

UNIQUE
Indicates that this creature is unique and has a personal rather than 
general name. Use this definition BEFORE defining the creatures name.

SPELLCASTING CREATURES
----------------------

MANA <X>
Defines the number of mana points a monster has.

SPELLUSE <% chance>
Defines the chance of the monster using its spells as opposed to its 
weapon or special attack.

SPELLSKILL <X>
Defines the base spellcraft skill the creature has.

CASTLEVEL <x>
Defines the duration of a creatures spell.

NONDISRUPTABLE
This indicates that the creature's spell will not be disrupted by an 
attack.

SILENCEIGNORE
This indicates that the Silence spell will not affect the creature's 
ability to cast a spell.

SPELL <Spell#>
Grants the creature the given spell. NOTE: Not all spells will work but
almost all offensive and defensive spells will work.

DEFINING SPECIAL ATTACKS
------------------------

Besides giving a creature spells to cast, you may give it a "special 
attack form" which is an attack that always works.

SPECUSE <%chance>
Defines the percent chance that the creature will choose to use its 
special attack in preference to other attacks.

SPECUSES <x>
Limits the creatures ability to use its special attack to x times.

SPECBASE <x>
Sets the <minimum> damage that this attack will do.

SPECDMG <x>
Sets the additional <random> damage range that this attack will do.

SPECDMGTYPE <Heat, cold, slash, electric>
Sets the damage type for special attack forms.

TEXX <special attack text output..>
This text assumes the following format. "%s blah blah blah %s." You must 
have the two %s strings in there. They have nothing to do with the echo 
command uses. They are replaced with the attacking creatures name and the 
victim creatures name respectively. do not include quotes in your 
definition. Example: TEXX %s breathes a gout of flame at %s!







REDEFINING the default text fields for a monster
------------------------------------------------

The text fields may be redefined by using the TEX? commands.
Example:
TEXH A thief fails to sink into the shadows.

Redefines the text for a certain creature that is shown when that creature
fails its % chance to hide. The available redefinitions are as follows.

TEXB   Text displayed when the creature is leading a group and its
       group arrives.
TEXC   Text displayed when the creature is leading a group and its
       group leaves the room.
TEXV   Text output when a creature prepares a spell when it is
       invisible.
TEXH   When the creature fails to hide.
TEXX   When the creature uses its special attack.
TEXE   Creature enters a room.
TEXI   When an attack is made with a weapon that is not enchanted
       powerfully enough to affect the monster.
TEXQ   When  the creature fails its spellcraft check.
TEXZ   A text field output once and a while when the monster is in
       the room with a dead person. Somewhere in this string must be
       a %s(has nothing to do with the ECHO usage %s) which indicates
       that the name of the creature who is to be the target of the
       monster's mockery should be replaced there.
TEX1  \
TEX2   \ These define some text which can be output once and a while
TEX3   / selected randomly. They provide you with the ability to
TEX4  /  animate your creatures.

TEXD   Text output when the creature is killed.
TEXF   Output when the creature flees. Don't add extra spaces or
       punctuation on the end of this line, since the game needs to
       append the direction of movement. For example:
       TEXF A goblin scrambles
       Might generate "A goblin scrambles north."
TEXM   Output when the creature moves. Like TEXF, don't add extra
       spaces or punctuations on the end.
TEXG   Output when the creature is generated in the room.
TEXS   Output when the creature prepares a spell.
TEXL   Output when the creature gestures (casts its spell).
TEXA   When the creature advances. Similar to TEXF and TEXM don't add
       extra spaces or punctuations.
TEXR   When the creature retreats.
