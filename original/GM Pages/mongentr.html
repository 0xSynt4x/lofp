Poison: Enter the probability that a hit will result in poisoning, followed by the level of poison delivered. If no poison, enter 0. EX: 50 80 would mean your critter would deliver poison 50 percnet of the time at level 80 POISON [% chance] [x]
If this is defined, then the player has %chance of being poisoned by a strike. Player will lose x body per minute where x=x-1: i.e.
level 20 poison -- first minute -20 bp
second minute 19 bp
third minute 18 bp
etc
DISEASE: Enter the probability that a hit will result in disease, followed by the level of disease delivered. If no disease, enter 0. Ex: 50 90 would mean your critter would deliver disease 50 percent of the time at level 90. See POISON above for a description of how Disease works.
FATIGUE : Drains fatigue. Enter the probability that a hit will result in fatigue drain, followed by the level of drain. EX: 30 100 You critter would drain 100 fatigue on 30 percent of hits. Unlike poison and disease, fatigue is a one-time drain on fatigue points remaining.

Adding Psionics
The following fields will allow you to give your critter psionic capabilities.
Assign the psi points your creature has. Enter a whole number. If your creature will not have psionic attacks, enter 0. Be careful to assign enough psi to maintain all assigned disciplines.
How often will your creature choose a psionic attack? Enter the percentage of time that your creature will opt to use a psionic attack as opposed to magic or weapons.
Enter the creature's psi skill level. Powerful psionics creatures should have well over 100 in order to resist players' natural resistance to psi attacks. This number defines the total chance a psionics creature has when invoking psi power.
Determine your creature's level of skill. This number determines your creature's effectiveness in the Matter disciplines i.e. -- Thrust, Cryogenics, Pyrogenics.
Assign psi disciplines to your monster. CTRL-CLICK to enter multiple disciplines. Gives the creature the psionic discipline # you specify.
