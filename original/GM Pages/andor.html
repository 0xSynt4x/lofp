GM Manual - Andor

Way back when, in the age when those that walked Andor are now referred to as `older than dirt', there was a document that gave background information on the world in general and even parts of the solar system. The original document file is long gone, but a copy was dusted off and its contents recreated here.

The Planet

Legends of Future Past takes place on the planet Andor. This name hasn't been that commonly used in conversation, but is increasing. "The Realms", which refers to the area extending roughly about 200 miles away from Fayd, was intended to be the most common term, but it never quite worked out that way. It would be constructive to start to reinstate that term, since we're going to be expanding beyond that in the near future. The surface of the planet is approximately seventy-nine percent water. There are twin polar ice caps and three continents. About seventy seven percent of the landmass is arid desert, wasteland or tundra. Pockets of life still flourish in places however, brilliant oases separated by endless leagues of dead land. The living land is slowly retaking the desert, increasing total fertile land by about one percent per year.

The Calendar and Astrology of the Realms

There are three hundred forty three days in the Andoran year. There is some debate over what this number represents. The Church of Shemri used to teach that Andor was the center of the universe and that it took three hundred forty three days for the sun to revolved around it. Since the Siryx debacles, however, when several notable members of the faith, namely Strom O'Berin and Cartin Greycloak, were in spaceships in the solar system, that teaching has been quietly shelved. The Rorinites have always taught the opposite, that the sun was the center around which things revolved. Regardless of which teaching is believed, the number of days in which the revolutions takes place is three hundred forty three, it's just a matter of which body revolves around which.

The year is divided into twelve months of twenty eight days each (4 weeks of 7 days). Each month represents the time it takes for the Great Moon to revolve around the world. This leaves seven days outside of the months which are used in the beginning of every year for the New Year's celebration known as the Feast of the Great Moon. It is during these Feast Days that annual agreements are renewed, treaties reaffirmed, etc. The months, in order, are Abra, Brama, Manta, Dretmar, Alabea, Phobrus, Melma, Banamea, Olum, Mixus, Farnum and Folster.

Altogether, there are three moons that revolve around Andor. The largest is the golden-hued Great Moon which appears about the size of a saucer. The next largest moon is called Phulcus, a dull-gray disk about half the size of the Great Moon. The smallest is called Shakar, the Crimson Moon, which appears as a small red dot. It takes twenty eight days for the Great Moon to make its orbit, approximately forty one days for Phulcus and a mere three days for Shakar. There is a conjunction of Phulcus and the Great Moon every two hundred forty days, of Phulcus and Shakar every fifty-nine days, of Shakar and the Great Moon every forty five days and of all three moons every two thousand four hundred nineteen days (seven years, eighteen days). NOTE: These conjunctional figures are going to be re-evaluated, don't take them as gospel.

The locations and current phase of the three moons are the center of Andoran astrology. For example, the night that Shakar is full but the Great Moon and Phulcus are new is known as the Night of the Crimson moon, a night of ill-omen. A child born on that night is often considered to be evil. Fortunately, this lunar combination occurs only once every five years. The accusation, " You were born under the Crimson Moon," is considered a powerful epithet. On the other hand, to be born during the conjunction of Phulcus and the Great Moon is considered to be an extraordinarily good omen. Children born during this time are usually encouraged to enter the clergy. To know all the combinations of the moons and their meaning is an art.

Eclipses also have significance. Only the Great moon experiences a noticeable lunar eclipse, turning a reddish-orange color during the event. Known as the "Bleeding Moon" it is a considered a harbinger of bad-fortune in the areas where it appears as a full eclipse (about once every seventy years per location). A partial eclipse of the sun can occur with both Phulcus and the Great Moon _ once every six years per location. A total eclipse of the sun is possible only with the Great Moon and occurs once every two hundred forty years per location. The meaning of a solar eclipse is different for different people (for example, the worshippers of Rorin see it as an open of Armageddon).

The Royal Family

The royal family consists currently of Zorha, the queen mother, mother of Frederick IV and Liriama. Frederick is currently 20, his birthday being Folster 8th, 1019. Liriama is 14, her birthday is Phobrus 1st, 1025. Frederick's real name was Radan, which he gave up when he assumed the throne. Although many dark issues surrounded Fred in the past, he has now `seen the light' and is a good and noble king.
