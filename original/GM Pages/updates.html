New Races

Date: Monday, October 10, 1994  4:07pm                      Electronic Mail

** Effective immediately, the following aliens have landed:

Any scripts using IFVAR RAC ! 9 to guarantee a "true" if/endif clause
must be altered, as races 9+ will soon be implemented. As you might imagine,
the downfall of all humanity might occur if a player of RAC 9 or 10 were to
not trigger a certain IFVAR RAC somewhere in the game. (I think 9 will, in
fact, be used to represent vampires, but that's unofficial.)

If you need to change any IFVAR RAC ! 9's, change them to IFVAR PLRNUM ] 0
or IFVAR RAC ] 0.

Date:    10/20/94 at 15:49:38 EST

The "Inorganic" Flag will allow us to create creatures whose physical
makeup is mechanical, or otherwise...inorganic.

Currently, inorganics cannot eat or drink, (and they don't need to
sleep, either!) They cannot be healed via the Healing skill, but can
automatically repair themselves and other, similar inorganics. They
are immune to BR and BD spells and will eventually include a host of
other immunities and vulnerabilities.

I know, it's so exciting, you can hardly hold your water. But still,
try it out Friday and see for yourself!

New Room Changes

Sometimes we'll script rooms with exits that the players can't actually get through. An example is follows: 
NUMBER 2600
NAME Jewelry City
EXIT N 2600
IFPREVERB N -1
 CLEARVERB
 ECHO PLAYER Sorry, you don't have a damn membership card!
ENDIF
With the addition of the new SCAN command, you might think, "Oh, gee, if someone scans to the north, they're going to see room 2600." Not so! This has been taken care of most thoughtfully. What you do have to worry about now is rooms that take the following form: 
NUMBER 2600
NAME Mal's World, Somewhere
EXIT D 590
IFPREVERB D -1
 CLEARVERB
 ECHO PLAYER You can't seem to find a way down here.
ENDIF
.
NUMBER 590
NAME Players suck!
Something like that is used in Mal's world to allow monsters to go to a buffer room where they are destroyed. If your scripts contain any such rooms, then you can prevent players from scanning into them with the following line:

NAME NOSCAN
