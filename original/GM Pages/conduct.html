GM Manual - Conduct

Professionalism

As a GM you should act professionally, maintaining a calm, civil manner and doing your best to answer questions, settle disputes and assist players without embarrassing yourself, other GM's or the game.

Discretion

Players and GM's value their privacy, and expect you to keep their identities and other secrets private from other players. You should not reveal facts about other players, nor discuss their behavior, in an out of character fashion. In addition, there are many, many facts about the game and its world which are best left for the player to discover for themselves.

Communications

Be courteous when speaking with GM's and players. Even if a player becomes abusive, you can still act professionally and not sink to their level. Stay calm. There's nothing wrong with making any of the following statements:

"I'm sorry, I can't do that."
"I'm sorry, I don't know the answer to that. I'll try to find out."
"That is something you should ask about in character. Ask another player."
"I'm not qualified to do that. Please ask another GM."
"If you continue to behave like this, you can be suspended or terminated."

Settling Disputes

You are always relied upon to use your best judgement. That includes deciding that the best course of action is to refer a problem to another GM or management, making a judgement call yourself or asking another GM for advice. Do what is best for the game, GM's and other players while trying to keep the customer happy. If a player asks for something that cannot be performed or provided, suggest alternatives. It's better to say, "How about this?" instead of just saying, "No!"
