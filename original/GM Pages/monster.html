Monster Scripting

Monster Adjectives

Adjectives for monsters are difined similar to, but not the same as they are for items. Everyone must be assigned numbers for monster adjectives. To define a monster adjective you use MADJDEF # [adjective].

Example:

    MADJDEF 10 giant
To initiate a monster block you state MNUMBER #. Once again you must be assigned Monster numbers.

Monsters

NAME [name text]

    Defines the name for the monster. Do not use a capital letter to start the monsters name unless you are making it a UNIQUE monster.

ADJECTIVE #

    Defines the adjective for the monster. Do not use Adjectives with Unique monsters. This number is defined by the MADJ(See monsterAdjectives above).

HUMAN

    This is a macro that defines the creatures body parts as humanoid.

AVINE

    Defines the creatures body parts as birdlike.

ANIMAL

    Defines a creatures body parts as a quadripedal animal.

BODY #

    Defines the number of body points that are factored into the exp value of the monster.

EXTRABODY

    Defines an added amount of body point between 50-100% of the actual number that is NOT factored into the exp value of the monster.

ATTACK1 #

    Defines the monsters attack modifier of the creatures primary attack.

ATTACK2 #

    Defines the attack modifier of the creatures secondary attack. This is not used at the moment.

DEFENSE #

    Defines the monsters defend modifier.

STRATEGY #

    This defines how the monster reacts in combat and overall behavior. The following strategies are available.
0011-100 Non-hostile, flee when attacked. (note: if 1-25, the
         creature will also cry for help and alert legal authorities)

101-200  Non-hostile, defend themselves when attacked. 101-125 will
         cry for help and alert legal authorities.

201-300  Animals that always flee even if not attacked but defend
         themselves if attacked.

301-400  Hostile, flee when wounded.  (group A)
401-500  Hostile, flee when wounded.  (group B)
501-600  Hostile, fight to the death. (group A)
601-700  Hostile, fight to the death. (group B)

There is no destinction between group A and B at this time.
The intention  of the difference in the future will be that
Group A and group B will be mutually hostile.

TREASURE #

    Defines the treasure level for the creature. If this is zero, the creature has no treasure.

IMMUNITY (immunity #) (immunity level)

    This defines a monster has having immunity to certain forms of attack. Or in some cases extra susceptability to certain forms of attack. Immunity level defines how susceptable or immune it is to that form of attack in half-units. 2 is the default setting which means the creature is affected 2/2 (in full). 1 would be half damage. 3 would be 1 and 1/2 damage and 4 would be double damage. All immunities, unless noted otherwise, are set to the default 2 except for #8 which is set to 0.
The available immunities are:

0 - Ensnaring attacks, particularly large or supernatural creatures
    would ignore this form of attack.

1 - Crushing weapons.

2 - Edged weapons.

3 - Heat attacks.

4 - Electrical attacks.

5 - Cold attacks.

6 - Nervous system attacks. Creature would be immune to spells like
    charm, sleep, psionic blast, etc. due to the lack of a normal
    nervous system.

7 - Life affecting. creatures would be immune to body destruction,
    poison, disease and body restoration due to a lack of an active
    "life force" or  are to unlike normal
    life.

8 - Undead affecting. This is the only immunity that is default to 0
    and it must be set to a special value. When set to 3 it defines a
    creature as a lesser undead, and includes creatures up to and
    including a lesser wight. Setting it to 2 defines it as a greater
    undead such as a mummy, lesser vampire, etc. Setting it to 1
    should be reserved for extremely powerful undead such as a lich,
    a greater vampire, etc.

9 - Susceptability to death criticals. If set to 0 the creature would
    not receive a fancy death critical. This should be given to any
    creature that lacks a normal humanoid or animal physiology.

WEAPON (weapon#) (adjective#) (weighted probability)

    This defines the type of weapon the creature uses. Weapon# is the archetype # for the weapon. Adjective defines a standard adjective for the weapon and the weighted probability is the percent out of 100 that the creature attacks with this weapon.

WEAPONPLUS #

    Defines the creatures weapon as magical with # being the bonus of the weapon. For obvious reasons you want to be carefull not to set this value

    ARMOR #

      Defines the percentage value of damage reduction that the monster receives.

    MAGICWEAPON (1, 2 or 3)

      If this is defined, then a magic weapon is needed to damage the creature. If MAGICWEAPON 1, then any magic weapon will do. 2 requires a bonus of at least +10. 3 requires a bonus of at least +21.

      DISCOPORATE

        This indicates that if the monster is killed it will not leave a corpse behind.

      ETERNAL

        This indicates that the creature will not be unloaded by the system until it is killed.

      GENDER [0, 1 or 2]

      0 - male,
1 - female
2 - neutral (defualt)

      STEALABLE

        This indicates the monster may be stolen from.

      POISON [% chance] [x]

        If defined, this provides a percent chance that a level x poison is delivered to the victim upon being struck.

      DISEASE [% chance] [x]

        This is basically the same as POISON.

      FATIGUE [%chance] [x]

        This is the equivalent of a life draining attack.

      RESIST [x]

        This defines the creatures magic resistance.

      SPEED [x]

        The default setting is 3. It defines how fast the creature attacks and moves. 1 is fastest and 4 or more is for extremely slow creatures.

      HIDESKILL [X]

        This defines a percentage chance that the creature will be able to hide. it is also used to define the creatures skill at backstab. X is the base chance. Warning: do not define X above 100.

      GUARD [X]

        defines what creature this monster will guard where X is the monster number that is to be guarded.

      UNIQUE

        Indicates that this creature is unique and has a personal rather than general name. Use this definition BEFORE defining the creatures name.

      Spell Casting

      MANA [X]

        Defines the number of mana points a monster has.

      SPELLUSE [% chance]

        Defines the chance of the monster using its spells as opposed to its weapon or special attack.

      SPELLSKILL [X]

        Defines the base spellcraft skill the creature has.

      CASTLEVEL [x]

        Defines the duration of a creatures spell.

      NONDISRUPTABLE

        This indicates that the creature's spell will not be disrupted by an attack.

      SILENCEIGNORE

        This indicates that the Silence spell will not affect the creature's ability to cast a spell.

      SPELL [Spell#]

        Grants the creature the given spell. NOTE: Not all spells will work but almost all offensive and defensive spells will work.

      Psionics

        Defines the creature's psionic resistance. If you don't specify any, it will default to the same as its normal magic resistance. (Note: This is a monsters resistance to mental attacks, ie stuns).

      PSI [x]

        The number of psi points the creature has.

      DISCIPLINE [x]

        Gives the creature the psionic discipline # you specify.

      PSIUSE [x]

        Specifies a percentage (1-100) of the time that this creature will opt

        PSISKILL [x]

          The total chance that the psionic creature has when invoking apsionic power. Powerful psionic creatures should have well overa 100 so as to defeat a player's natural resistance to psionics.

        PSILEVEL [x]

          The creature's effective skill level in the Mind over Matter skill. This is used for determining the effectiveness of Cryokinetics, Pyrokinetics, Electrify and Kinetic Thrust.

        How to design a good psionic creature

        Psionic creatures should always have at least one psionic attack discipline for overcoming a psionicist's defense modes; it should also have a defense mode of its own. Then just give it whatever you think it should have. It will probably take some testing to find out what monsters can use well. I've tried to build in some intelligence for monsters so that they'll make wise selections from the disciplines you give them based on their situation.

        One difference with psionic creatures v. magic creatures: if you give them a defense discipline (be it a mental defense or the physical defenses), they get genned with those disciplines already active. Make sure you give psionic monsters enough psi to maintain whatever they have.

        Don't give monsters non-battle psionic disciplines (like levitation, flight, strengthen steel, etc.). Don't give them Domination either, because they aren't smart enough to make use of it properly (at this time anyway).

        Defining Special Attacks

        Besides giving a creature spells to cast, you may give it a "special attack form" which is an attack that always works.

        SPECUSE [%chance]

          Defines the percent chance that the creature will choose to use its special attack in preference to other attacks.

        SPECUSES [x]

          Limits the creatures ability to use its special attack to x times.

        SPECBASE [x]

          Sets the [minimum] damage that this attack will do.

        SPECDMG [x]

          Sets the additional [random] damage range that this attack will do.

        SPECDMGTYPE [Heat, cold, slash, electric]

          Sets the damage type for special attack forms.

        TEXX [special attack text output..]

          This text assumes the following format. "%s blah blah blah %s." You must have the two %s strings in there. They have nothing to do with the echo command uses. They are replaced with the attacking creatures name and the victim creatures name respectively. do not include quotes in your definition.
        Example: TEXX %s breathes a gout of flame at %s!

        Redifining the Defualt text fields for monsters

          The text fields may be redefined by using the TEX? commands.
        Example: TEXH A thief fails to sink into the shadows.
          Redefines the text for a certain creature that is shown when that creature fails its % chance to hide. The available redefinitions are as follows.
        TEXB   Text displayed when the creature is leading a group and its
       group arrives.
TEXC   Text displayed when the creature is leading a group and its
       group leaves the room.
TEXV   Text output when a creature prepares a spell when it is
       invisible.
TEXH   When the creature fails to hide.
TEXX   When the creature uses its special attack.
TEXE   Creature enters a room.
TEXI   When an attack is made with a weapon that is not enchanted
       powerfully enough to affect the monster.
TEXQ   When  the creature fails its spellcraft check.
TEXZ   A text field output once and a while when the monster is in
       the room with a dead person. Somewhere in this string must be
       a %s(has nothing to do with the ECHO usage %s) which indicates
       that the name of the creature who is to be the target of the
       monster's mockery should be replaced there.

TEX1  \
TEX2   \ These define some text which can be output once and a while
TEX3   / selected randomly. They provide you with the ability to
TEX4  /  animate your creatures.

TEXD   Text output when the creature is killed.
TEXF   Output when the creature flees. Don't add extra spaces or
       punctuation on the end of this line, since the game needs to
       append the direction of movement.
       For example:  TEXF A goblin scrambles Might generate "A
       goblin scrambles north."

TEXM   Output when the creature moves. Like TEXF, don't add extra
       spaces or punctuations on the end.
TEXG   Output when the creature is generated in the room.
TEXS   Output when the creature prepares a spell.
TEXL   Output when the creature gestures (casts its spell).
TEXA   When the creature advances. Similar to TEXF and TEXM don't add
       extra spaces or punctuations.
TEXR   When the creature retreats.
