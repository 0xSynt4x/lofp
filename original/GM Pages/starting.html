GM Manual

Getting Started

There are several things you'll need as a GM. You need a mentor to help teach them the ropes, answer questions and evaluate your progress. See Gary if he hasn't assigned one yet. You'll also need a GM account, access rights to the GM web site and GM privileges in the message boards and calendar.

An important thing you need to know as a new GM is how to conduct yourself. A GM needs to demonstrate discretion, fairness, honesty and integrity above all, along with the talent for storytelling and fun that got you selected as a GM in the first place. The chapter on Conduct will familiarize you with how to behave with players and other GMs. We perform a service, and we want to please our customers within reason so they enjoy themselves and keep coming back for more.

As a GM, you need to know the history and geography of the game, along with a myriad of other facts that you can find in the References section.

Once you're armed with the knowledge and behavior of a GM, you're ready to actually act as a GM. At first, you'll have limited powers and access. After a probationary period (typically 30 days or until your mentor says you're ready) you'll be given more privileges. To perform as a GM, you'll need to become familiar with the GM Commands, which are an extension of the basic command verbs you're used to. All GM commands begin with the @ 'at' sign, such as: @GO 206.
