Example:
  analyze mandrake root (Test for alchemical properties)
  brew mandrake root in flask (You begin brewing)
  brew babich root in flask (You continue brewing)
  brew muur crystal in flask (You finish brewing)
  pour potion in cauldron, put clay in cauldron (make creme)

Bad recipes give a foul odor. If it shimmers magically, you made a good potion! If it bubbles violently, it's a good recipe, but beyond your current skill. To make a potion you must have as much alchemy skill as you would need to cast the related spell. You can start several potions at once and brew them all in stages. You can start a brew and have another alchemist complete it. Brewing order is not important. Potions brewed will yield 2 to 5 sips.

Alchemy is not needed to combine or extend potions. A potion poured into another potion of the same type (even if they appear different) will combine, but change appearance. A potion can sometimes be extended by adding it to a different type (even BD). This will yield: the original potion, the added potion, an entirely different potion, a BD potion, a useless potion, or an explosion (destroys flask). It's recommended that a container be filled with a cheap, easy or BD potion, one sip taken from it, and then filled from the desired potion. Pouring can overflow a container, filling can't.
