Spells Explanations

contributed by Ilhana [UID:Corey] 
Magic in 1001 easy lessons.

Magic on Andor is a complex subject, taking a little time to
understand and a lot to master. I've put together some notes
answering the questions I'm most commonly asked about the use
of spells below. I don't claim it's complete, but I hope that
it's of use...

Ilhana Loereili D'Chessenta, known as Ashimi
Elder of the Silver Arcana

[It also hasn't yet been edited, so please ignore
transcription errors.]

---------------------------------------------------------------

Part One: Introduction

I. The choice of your primary spells depends on style and
preference. Any mage who does not have a strong non-magic
skill (bow, melee weapon, psi) will eventually need at least
two heavily mastered spells with which to hunt. Contrary to
rumor, they need not be in conjuration.

II. Flexibility among schools is useful, but not an utter
necessity. It is possible, by harvesting or buying herbs, to
forego necromancy entirely. Druidic is a useful school, but
nice rather than a necessity. Enchantment is more difficult to
ignore, as eventually one either needs more defense than is
easily possible or the spells "Wizard's Armor", "Haste",
"Slow" and "Flight" to easily survive more difficult areas,
but it can be done. Conjuration can be foregone entirely, but
is has the most useful offensive and defensive array of spells
possible. It is better to be good in your school of choice
than knowledgeable, but not concentrated, in all of them.

III. The Schools

        A. Conjuration: A school strong on offensive and
        defensive spells, containing all the basic elemental
        attacks and the ability to summon guardians
        (elementals).

        B. Enchantment: An "enhancing" school containing
        spells that make transportation, hand-to-hand fighting
        and life easier. Can be used for defense against
        non-physical attacks and in emergencies, escape.

        C. Necromancy: A school of both healing and
        destruction, necromancy's offensive capabilities are
        strongly underrated at the time I write this.

        D. Druidic: A school that provides protection against
        the vaugaries of the elements, tools to free oneself
        from those who would trap you, some defense and a
        great deal of aid to those who use a bow.

IV. Mastery - "Mastering" a spell is the process of studying a
spell closely in order to learn its ins and outs. The cost is
8 builds for the first rank of mastery and 4 for each
additional rank.

Any mastery reduces casting time from three seconds to two.
Mana cost is lessened by two for each rank of mastery down to
a minimum of half the original cost of the spell, rounded
down. Damage, on offensive spells, is increased by around 1.5
points per rank.

"Minimum mastery" is when you have a spell mastered enough
times for minimum mana cost (E.g., Freezing Sphere, a 9th rank
spell, is minimally mastered at 3 ranks to a cost of 4 mana.)
"Full mastery" is when you have mastered a spell to the
maximum extent allowed - the level of the spell + one. (E.g.,
Freezing Sphere, mastered 10 times.)

Part Two: Offense

I. After a certain point (early on, yet) the key to
effective use of magic is mastery. The spells first mastered
should be offensive. A list of the most useful candidates
follows:

Conjuration     Enchantment     Necromancy      Druidic

Force Blade     Dancing Sword   Spectral Fist   Earth Spike
                                Spectral Sword

Lightning Bolt                  Destroy Undead II
Freezing Sphere                 Destroy Undead III
Flaming Sphere                  Body Destruction III

Flaming Arrows

Whatever your school of choice, pick a low-mana, high-utility
spell (force blade, spectral fist, earth spike) early on and
fully master it. The result will be a weapon that is low cost
- one or two mana - that does a decent amount (usually
3-24/3-30) of damage. Start on this as soon as you get 12
spellcraft.

Other spells can be mastered to whatever degree you prefer -
there are advantages to having minimum mastery (defined as
"enough mastery to cut the mana cost in half") of several
spells, especially if you like being able to move around. But
having one or two fully mastered spells can make for
impressive results in your chosen hunting areas.

II. Some examples of workable mastery combinations:

Force Blade & Freezing Sphere - although a fire weapon is
useful, full mastery of both of these spells will "take care
of you" through most on Andor. It's the basic conjurer's
toolkit, and incredibly useful in the mid-level mages
playground - the wastes.

Lightning Bolt & Force Blade
                 Earth Spike
                 Destroy Undead

Lightning Bolt is a spell that must be fully mastered to get
any appreciable effect on creatures not suspectible to
electricity. However, you can "work through" to those as
several of the low-to-mid level creatures, such as those in
the Swamp and Ursine Magi, light up quite nicely. At upper
levels, it is a very useful spell on the Astral Planes:
rollers, robots, terminators and fleshblighters all react to
it quite well. It is entirely possible to hunt the wastes with
lightning, although not quite spectacularly as someone with
heavily-mastered freezing sphere.

I list it with Earth Spike because it's a good style of spell
expensive and rarely used as it requires being outdoors in a
heavy rain.

Destroy Undead & Earth Spike - Necromancy and Druidic are a
popular combination. These two spells give a nice basic weapon
against non-undead, and an extremely powerful one against one
of the most common enemies of life on and off Andor. There are
several advantages to Destroy Undead, including the fact that
there is no such thing as a "hand shot" when you are attacking
their very essence and the fact backfires won't hurt you
unless you're a vampire. The second rank is worth mastering,
but if you are a serious necromancer I recommend only getting
the minimum mastery - fully mastered DUIII is a deadly weapon
and worth waiting for.

Destroy Undead & Spectral Fist & Spectral Sword - For the pure
necromancer, the answer to "how do I do it?" is "Master
everything." Spectral fist will blast your way through greater
skeletons and the lesser live creatures of Andor, if you're
patient, while Spectral Sword fully mastered will give you a
mid-cost solution to the "live opponent" woes.

Dancing Sword - I do not recommend pure enchantment to anyone.
Period. But if you must, fully master Dancing Sword
immediately. It will be your only weapon, and it is a useful
one.

Force Blade & Lightning Bolt & Freezing Sphere & Flaming
Sphere - the four basic weapons of the conjurer, these spells
will get you through anything, anywhere.

Upper level mastery for a conjurer usually includes flaming
arrows (for those pesky Underkeep deizens), a druid who hunts
the planes might play with Call Lightning if his hunting
partners don't object to rusting weapons. A necromancer just
keeps adding in ranks to the Destroy Undead spell, in rotation
with other skills he or she wants.

III. Weaknesses and Immunities

"Blade" spells usually work well on all creatures except the
following: Skeletons, bone golems, undead heads. There are few
creatures suspectible to blades, but the bleeding induced can
be useful.

"Crushing" spells work best on skeletal creatures: skeletons,
bone golems.

Cold does not work on lesser undead: skeletons, ghouls,
greater ghouls, banshees (?), etc. Greater undead, such as
military ghouls, spectral lords and gnomen, are vunerable to
it. Cold creatures, such as frigils, rollers and so on, are
nearly invunerable to it. So are fleshblighters and the
occasional demon. Military ghouls, mechanical opponents (such
as robots and terminators), fire giants, sandmen, mounds and
stalkers all seem especially susceptible to chill.

Lightning is deadly to creatures of the Swamp, ursine
magicans, the creatures on the ice-world Xertaatal, robots and
terminators and scattered other creatures. Gnomen necromancers
are immune to lightning, but it usually does normal or
slightly reduced damage on almost anything else.

Fire works well on sharkor, frost giants, spectral lords,
gnomen necromancers, storm giants, the Darkoth and a few other
creatures. Chaos demons are often immune to heat, as are
astral stalkers.

Destroy Undead does NOT work on demons (obvious ones or less
obvious ones such as gnomen necromancers) or magical
constructs (bone golems.)

Part Three: Defense

There are two ways to approach defense as a spellcaster: you
can rely upon guards or upon yourself. The first is by far the
most popular and less costly approach. However, the second is
occasionally necessary.

I. Defending yourself

Defensive spells are available in every school but
enchantment. They are:

Mystic Armor (Conjuration, 5th rank) - +20 to defense.
Carapace (Druidic, 8th rank) - +20 to defense, caster only.
Spectral Shield (Necromacy, 9th rank) - +20 to defense.
Prismatic Globe (Conjuration, 15th rank) - +50 to defense.
Mass Protection (Conjuration, 23rd rank) - +25 to caster, +20 to
all joined.
Prismatic Globe II (Conjuration, 30th rank) +100 to defense.

However, the maximum defense with all spells and the
occasional defensive item will not protect a mage from any of
the more dangerous creatures in the Realms. The choice is
usually between learning other skills (psi or dodge) to
increase your defenses or working with a guard.

II. Guards

Guards are available in four varietes: summoned, controlled,
dominated or live.

Live is when you team with a psionic or fighter and let them
take the damage. It is most useful (and appreciated) when you
trade either defensive or healing spells for this service.
They usually make better conversationalists than the other
three varieties.

Conjurers can SUMMON an elemental guard at 12th rank. The
material components for these four spells are gemstones:
garnet (fire), tourmaline stone (earth), aquamarine (water)
and opal (air). An elemental will prevent all hand to hand
attacks by creatures employing non-magical weapons. Polearms,
spells and special attacks are another matter. Magical weapons
will kill an elemental, but you only encounter those at higher
levels (Skull House, the Black Towers.) High level conjurers
can use Tinderath's Chaotic Summons to get something
equivalent to, but better looking than, an elemental.

Enchanters can DOMINATE a creature to serve as guard. Lesser
werewolves from Grymwood are the best low-level guards, as
they have an immunity to non-magical weapons and are as
effective as elementals. There is the drawback of needing to
get your guard out of the area you're in - in winter, that's
quite a task. Bend Space II, at 24th rank, solves the problem,
but until then, be prepared to find your guard where you fight.
At higher levels, dominating a creature that's harder to hit
than the one that's hitting is the way to get around the fact
magical weapons WILL hit. A mantico with several protective
spells will serve as an effective guard in the Black Tower,
for example. Domination I is based on total body points - if
you're careful not to use a spell that induces bleeding, you
can beat a creature up until it will work. Domination II is
far more effective.

Necromancers can CONTROL undead. The drawback is that many
people consider it black magic. It's illegal in the city.
However, if the control is only for the purpose of a guard, it
can be considered a grey area - just make sure whoever sees
you thinks so too. Phantom caretakers are the working
equivalent of lesser werewolves. Spectral Lords and
highly-spelled gnomen would work similarly to the mantico
described above. Jolly Rogers are handy guards in Haven.
Control Undead is NOT based on body points, and beating a
spectral lord until it's laying on the floor still will not
allow Control Undead I or II to work. Black or grey
necromancers of 17th rank can SUMMON a greater wight, using
wight dust skinned from a lesser. These are ugly elementals.
Extremely skilled white necromancers have recourse to Spectral
Warriors at 32nd rank.

Druids are out of luck. Vipers are cute, but a rat could chew
them to bits.

III. Using a guard

When you are attacked by something that doesn't use a polearm,
simply command them to guard you. Polearm users can be
distracted (only one at a time, though, so be careful) by
commanding the elemental to slay the creature causing the
problem.

If your guard or familar is slain, you end up stunned. Be
careful of this.

Part Four: Spells More Useful than They Seem

I. Haste and Slow

These two spells, at the 5th rank of enchantment, are probably
the most useful in the books. HASTING a fighter turns him into
a killing machine, although he will soon be exhausted. For a
mage, haste is less useful because it cancels out mastery. But
as many creatures SLOW you throughout your adventuring career,
some ability to haste (whether a gallon of potion or the
spell) becomes a necessity.

However, the most useful application of SLOW is against
special attacks. Worm spit, mantico tails, robotic bullets and
so on can soon reduce the most able mage to a burned, bleeding
mess. Slow gives you the time to kill them first.

II. Wizard's Armor and Silence

Sooner or later, things will hit you. Polearms are
unavoidable. This spell stops your spells from being
disrupted, or lost, when you are hit. Silence keeps a mage
magic-using creatures (necromancers, rakashka, etc.) that
depend on hand movements or symbols. It is, however, very
handy when a sorcerous assassin comes after you.

III. Flight, Bend Space and Tree Door

A flight item (potion, wand, ring, etc.) or the ablitity to
cast the spell becomes a necessity once you begin exploring
outside of Fayd. Without 10 ranks of climbing, getting into
the Deep Realms or up into the Wastes without flight is a
deadly or at least bruising proposition. Even the bridge to
Elemental Giants (and the walk to Mirdaal) is a difficulty -
if you're carrying too much weight, you have to fly over the
bridge rather than walk. Flying is also a lot more convenient.

Bend Space is a teleportation spell, and the reason most mages
get lost the minute you ask them to go someplace unfamiliar.
It's faster than flying, but doesn't work in the grasslands,
Deep Realms or from the main land mass to the various islands.
(Haven, Crescent, Volcanic.)

Tree Door is effectively a "one way" teleportation spell for
druids. It only works where there is a tree to enter into, and
is very picky about which tree works. (Right outside of town
works, the uquart groves, the silver tree at Mirdaal, the
Hanging Tree in the Wastes and many others do not.) However,
you can (for example) get back from Mirdaal and the Wastes by
foot - and there is a tree along the grassland path on the
wastes - even though getting to them is much more difficult.
Handy.

IV. Body Restoration I

One rank of necromancy can be a very solid investment. The
first rank of body restoration (often used hasted to speed up
the process) is both lighter than babich root and easier to
use. The higher ranks of healing contain cure poison and
disease and BRIII... however, BRIII is so costly that it is
only really efficent if mastered or if you're fighting
creatures that can inflict frequent, massive damage.

V. Cold Shield, Heat Shield and Freedom

These are the three most useful spells in druidic magic. Cold
shield halves damage from heat, heat shield halves damage from
cold and freedom frees you from plant snare, webs, insect
swarms and tentacles. The protection offered allows you to
fight for far more time with less risk of death.

VI. Mystic Key

Locked doors and chests are a pain. This magical "skeleton
key" gives you the equivalent of 7 ranks of lockpicking, and
opens most doors that are meant to be opened easily. (Mirdaal,
Kvantar Tree, Keep gate, etc.)

VII. Spells useful to others...

Protection spells (see "Defense") for fighters and psis.
Strength increases a fighter's damage and skill. Agility does
the same for an archer, but most have recourse to a magical
fountain that can provide the same result. Healing spells are
useful to all, and invigoration can allow a fighter to keep
chopping almost continually, or a psi to drain their fatigue
endlessly.

Part Five: General Tips and Tricks

Submission - on occasion, you will need to cast an offensive
spell upon yourself. To do so, SUBMIT and cast. Then
immediately UNSUBMIT, or any hostile spellcaster can hit you
at will.

If you are webbed or entangled in plants, insects or
tentacles, there are several ways to get out.

        1. Cast Freedom
        2. Submit and cast "Dispel Lesser Magic." WARNING: You
        will lose a spell randomly at each cast. It may not be
        the web.
        3. Cast slime or mist form. (It is recommended you
        haste yourself first as transforming back takes a long
        time and you will be vunerable during it.) Wolflings
        can transform out of them as well.
        4. If the spell is damaging, but you are not otherwise
        at risk, submit and web yourself. This will get rid of
        the insect swarm or tentacles, but you'll be caught in
        your own web.

When fighting a creature (e.g., worms, gnomen) that take a
long time to kill, you may want to alternate your low-mana
spell (force blade, fist) with the more damaging high-mana
ones (sphere, destroy undead). This will let you fight a bit
longer between rests.

Using marks - Bends Space, Tree Door, Translocation and Scry
all involve the use of "marks" - up to 10 areas a mage has
studied and memorized. To mark a place, MARK 1 (or 2, through
10.) To review your marks, type MARK. To cast a spell to or at
a mark (to travel there or see what's going on) CAST [mark #].
E.g., to teleport to Bazaar Center:

First, type MARK 1 when you're in Center.
Second, walk somewhere and prepare bend space.
Third, CAST 1.

Try to always carry a healing root or mushroom, a maufra
lichen and a rowik berry. There will be times you are badly
injured, diseased or poisoned and out of mana and these will
be lifesavers.

"Multi-target" spells are used this way. You TARGET up to nine
creatures (for fire arrows, tentacles or lightning, four for
translocation) and then CAST... either at the creature that
can resist the least or at yourself. Creatures remain
targeted until dead.

Some spells cancel each other out. They are:

If you're hunting with other people, take the time to figure
out everyone's strengths and weaknesses and work together.
Teaming up can be far more effective than simply rushing to
kill first.

When all else fails - ask around. If the person is busy, ask
them if you can talk to them later, write them with your
question or if they have suggestions as to who might be able

When mastering a spell with multiple ranks, it is almost
always best to wait for the highest rank. Destroy Undead II
for a pure necromancer or necromancer/druid is the primary
exception, as minimum mastery will greatly help the mage get

Items or potions with the following spells are good emergency
devices: flight, invisibility, haste, mist form.

Most protective or enhancing spells are cumulative - if you
cast them several times, the duration of the spell is
increased each time, to a maximum of 28 hours. There are a few
spells that aren't: True Aim, Divine Blessing, Haste and Slow
are the most commonly used ones. If you need to maintain them
constantly, you can recast right before it runs out, it will
"reset" the duration from the beginning.

Wands and items are useable while silenced.

Knocking someone over and entangling them (it's safer to
entangle first, knock second) will prevent almost every
creature or person except a pure psi from being able to attack
you. Some, not all, psionic creatures can attack while down.
Tentacles & maelstorm, or web/snare and earthwave, are popular
ways to accomplish this. Not all creatures are webbable, and
insect swarm and plant snare only work in certain (limited)
circumstances.

Stunning a psionic creature or person slows them down quite a
bit.

Remember to have fun.
